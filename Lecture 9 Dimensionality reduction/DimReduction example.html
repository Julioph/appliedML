We can now use PCA to reduce these 9 dimensional data to three-dimensional principal components (PCs).

In [11]:
from sklearn.decomposition import PCA
pca = PCA(n_components=3, random_state=42)
X_pca = pca.fit_transform(X_normalised)

We can now put the PCs for all the examples into a data frame. We can look at the first several rows.

print('Explained variation per principal component: {}'.format(pca.explained_variance_ratio_))

Explained variation per principal component: [0.33983774 0.16913347 0.12972709]

This means that almost 34% of the variance in the data can be explained by PC1. PC1 and PC2 together explain almost 51% (34+17) of the variance. PC1, PC2, and PC3 together can explain almost 64% of the variance in the data. We can also say that we lost 36% of the information when we reduce the dimension of the data from 9 to 3; we lost 49% of the information when we reduce the dimension of the data from 9 to 2.
